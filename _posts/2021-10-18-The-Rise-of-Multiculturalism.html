In the Playing in the Dark: Whiteness and the Literary Imagination.

Moving beyond arguments for increased diversity, Morrison urged for a more complete examination of the way that America’s history of chattel slavery not only shaped the experience of slaves but indelibly affected “the mind, imagination, and behavior of masters.” In this way, Morrison underscored the importance of thinking critically about the canon, particularly to make visible ideological underpinnings of the collections of text that are used to shape the next generation of writers and thinkers.
