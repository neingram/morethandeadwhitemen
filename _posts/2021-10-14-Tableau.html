Data Visualizations

The following visualizations were created using Tableau and the dataset I created for this project. Each visualization is interactive, meaning that clicking on the filters associated with each visualization will modify what you see.


Want more insight? Explore the history and scholarship behind this project.


This visualization displays texts and authors that are assigned according to frequency. In “Texts by Assignment Frequency,” the colors align to each textbook. In “Authors by Assignment Frequency,” the colors indicate if the author is a BIPOC (Black, Indigenous, People of Color). Please note that all of these visualizations are interactive, meaning that you can modify the visualization yourself. Some guiding questions for these visualizations:

  • How do the top texts vary by textbook?
  • What is the proportion of top texts assigned written by a BIPOC author?
  • What about gender? How many of the top texts are written by women? By men?
  • How do these visualizations provide insight into whose stories public school students are most frequently exposed?

This interactive map visualization provides a snapshot of the national origin of authors included in the textbooks contained in the dataset. You can filter by author gender, as well as whether of not an author is a BIPOC. Some guiding questions for this visualization:

  • What is the vision of the world created by the literature to which students are exposed in these curricular materials?
  • Do you notice any trends in terms of author’s gender and national origin? That is, are more women authors affiliated with specific countries when compared to men?

These visualizations compare the demographics of the authors included in the textbooks under examination with the demographics of the district in which the textbooks are used to teach. Notice that although neither LAUSD nor NYDOE contain a majority of white students, the authors of the texts included in students’ textbooks are predominately white.

This visualization is concerned with gender parity in public school textbooks. Feel free to use the interactivity of the visualization to filter by gender, genre, textbook, grade level, or course to see how the visualization changes. Some guiding questions for this visualization:

  • Are there any courses where the gender breakdown is completely equal?
  • Are there any courses that assigns more female authors than male authors?
  • What might the gender composition of texts assigned reveal or communicate?
  • What do you notice about the intersection of genre and gender?
